⚽ Sports & Fun Games! 🏀

A fun speaking lesson for Kids (A1 - B1)

🎯 What will we learn today?

  • Learn cool action words and sports vocabulary.
  • Talk about your favorite games and sports.
  • Share how you feel about winning, losing, and playing with friends!

🔥 1. Let's Warm Up!

Time to talk! Answer these fun questions. Click the button to see a cool example.

⚽ What is your favorite sport to watch or play?

"My favorite sport is football! I love kicking the ball really far."

☀️ Do you like playing inside or outside better?

"I like playing outside because I can run very fast!"

🏃‍♂️ Who do you usually play games with?

"I usually play games with my brother and my best friend."

📺 Do you watch any sports on TV?

"Yes, sometimes I watch basketball with my dad on Sundays."

🏓 Do you have any sports equipment at home (like a ball, bat, or bike)?

"Yes, I have a red bicycle and a basketball in my garage."

📚 2. Cool Sports Words!

Let's learn these sports words. Click the button to see how to use them!

1. To kick (verb) 🦵

To hit a ball hard with your foot.

"I like to kick the ball into the net!"
2. Team (noun) 🤝

A group of people who play a sport together.

"There are 11 players on a football team."
3. To score (verb) 💯

To get a point or a goal in a game.

"She is very fast and always scores points for us."
4. Coach (noun) 🧢

The teacher or boss of a sports team.

"Our coach tells us to run fast and drink lots of water."
5. Match (noun) 🏆

A sports competition or game against another team.

"We have a big tennis match on Saturday morning."
6. To practice (verb) ⏱️

To do something many times so you get better at it.

"I need to practice swimming before the summer."
7. Champion (noun) 🥇

The person or team who wins the whole tournament.

"My sister won a gold medal, she is a champion!"
8. Referee (noun) 🦓

The person who makes sure players follow the rules.

"The referee blew the whistle to stop the game."

🗣️ 3. Time to Talk!

Take turns answering these questions. Use the buttons to get cool ideas or see example answers!

Easy 🟢

1. What is your absolute favorite sport to play?

Ideas: Football, Basketball, Swimming, Badminton, Hide and Seek.
Short: "My favorite sport is badminton."
Long: "My favorite sport is badminton. I love hitting the shuttlecock over the net. It makes me feel very energetic!"
Easy 🟢

2. Do you prefer team sports or playing by yourself?

Ideas: Team is fun, working together / Alone is quiet, swimming, cycling.
Short: "I prefer team sports because it's fun."
Long: "I prefer team sports! I like playing football because I can pass the ball to my friends and we can win together as a team."
Easy 🟢

3. What game do you usually play at break time in school?

Ideas: Tag, Catch, skipping rope, playing on the swings, talking.
Short: "At break time, I play tag."
Long: "At break time, my friends and I always play tag in the school yard. It is really fun to run away and try not to get caught!"
Medium 🟡

4. Tell me about a time you won a game. How did you feel?

Ideas: Board game, school race, happy, proud, jumped up and down.
Short: "I won a running race last week and I felt very happy."
Long: "Last month, my team won the football match at school. I scored the last goal! I felt so proud and my whole team jumped up and down."
Medium 🟡

5. What do you do when it is raining and you can't play outside?

Ideas: Play video games, board games (Monopoly), read, watch TV, build Lego.
Short: "When it rains, I stay inside and play video games."
Long: "If it's raining, I usually play board games with my family. Our favorite game is Monopoly. Sometimes I also just read a book in my room."
Medium 🟡

6. Who is the best sports player you know? (Friend, family, or famous!)

Ideas: Messi, Ronaldo, my older brother, my dad, Steph Curry.
Short: "The best player I know is Lionel Messi."
Long: "The best player I know is my older brother. He plays basketball for his high school. He is very tall and practices every single day to get better."
Medium 🟡

7. If you could invent a brand new sport, what would it be like?

Ideas: Flying with jetpacks, using balloons, running in water, trampolines.
Short: "I would invent a sport where people play basketball on trampolines."
Long: "I would invent a sport called 'Balloon-ball'. It would be like volleyball, but you play on a giant trampoline and you use a huge balloon. You can't let the balloon touch the ground!"
Opinion 🔴

8. Is winning the most important thing when you play a game?

Ideas: No, having fun is better / Yes, I like to win / Trying your best matters.
Short: "No, I think having fun is the most important thing."
Long: "No, I don't think so. Winning is nice, but having fun and playing with your friends is much more important. If you lose, you can always try again next time."
Opinion 🔴

9. Do you think everyone should get a trophy, even if they lose?

Ideas: Yes, it makes people happy / No, only the winner should get one.
Short: "Yes, because it shows they tried their best."
Long: "I think everyone should get a small medal for participating because they practiced hard. But maybe the big golden trophy should only go to the champion."
Opinion 🔴

10. Does playing sports make you a better student in school? Why?

Ideas: Yes, keeps you healthy, gives you energy / Teaches you teamwork.
Short: "Yes, because it gives you energy to focus."
Long: "Yes, I strongly believe that. Playing sports keeps your body healthy and gives you energy. It also teaches you how to work in a team, which helps when doing group projects in class."

🎭 4. Let's Act It Out!

Choose a partner and practice these fun situations.

Scenario A: Let's Play!

Student A: You really want to play basketball at the park. You invite your friend.

Student B: You are tired and want to stay inside to play video games instead.

  • "Hey, do you want to go to the park and play basketball?"
  • "I'm a bit tired today. Can we play Minecraft inside?"
  • "But it's so sunny outside! We can just play for 30 minutes."
  • "Okay, fine! But you have to teach me how to shoot."

Scenario B: Don't Be Sad!

Student A: You just missed the final kick in your football match and your team lost. You are very sad.

Student B: You are Student A's teammate. You want to cheer them up and make them happy.

  • "I'm so sorry, guys. I ruined the game for us."
  • "Don't worry! You played great today."
  • "But if I scored, we would be the champions."
  • "It's just a game. Let's practice tomorrow and win next time!"

Today's Mission Goals:

  • Learn and use fun vocabulary about studying and habits.
  • Talk fluently about how you like to learn (watching, reading, doing).
  • Share your own opinions clearly and confidently.

1. Warm-Up Chats 🎈

Let's get our brains ready! Answer the questions below.

1. What is your absolute favorite subject at school? 🏫
"I love Science because we get to do fun experiments!"
2. Do you like learning new things? Why or why not? 🧐
"Yes, I do! It is very fun to know things that I didn't know yesterday."
3. Who usually helps you with your homework? 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦
"My mom usually helps me, especially with Math."
4. Where is your favorite place to study? 🛋️
"I like to study at the kitchen table so I can eat snacks."
5. Do you prefer reading books or watching videos to learn? 📺📚
"I prefer watching videos because they show me exactly how to do it."

2. Word Power! 📚

Learn these words to sound like a learning superstar.

Practice 🔁
Doing something again and again to get better.
"I practice playing the piano every afternoon."
Focus 🎯
To look at or think about one thing very carefully.
"I wear my headphones to help me focus on my reading."
Discover 🔭
To find something new that you didn't know before.
"We discovered a cool new insect in the garden today!"
Mistake ❌
Something you do wrong, which helps you learn.
"It is okay to make a mistake when you are learning to ride a bike."
Improve 📈
To get better at doing something.
"Reading every night helps me improve my English."
Curious 🤔
Wanting to know more and asking lots of questions.
"I am very curious about how spaceships fly."
Memory 🧠
Your brain's ability to remember things from the past.
"Playing matching games is great for your memory."
Explain 🗣️
To talk about something clearly so friends understand.
"Can you explain the rules of this game to me?"

3. Time to Speak! 🎙️

Try to answer these questions! Use the Idea Bank if you get stuck.

Easy
1. What is something new you learned how to do this week?
  • A new English word
  • A trick in a video game
  • How to cook a small snack
"I learned how to say 'hello' in Spanish."
"This week, I learned how to bake chocolate chip cookies. My mom taught me. I made a mistake with the sugar, but they still tasted good!"
Easy
2. What time of day do you learn best? Morning or night?
  • Morning: Brain is fresh, lots of energy.
  • Night: Very quiet, no distractions.
  • Afternoon: After eating a good snack.
"I learn best in the morning because I have lots of energy."
"I think I learn best at night before bed. The house is very quiet so I can easily focus on my reading. It helps my memory."
Easy
3. Do you like learning by yourself or with friends?
  • Friends: We can play games, it is funny.
  • By myself: I can be fast, it is quiet.
"I like learning with friends because it is fun."
"I prefer learning with friends. If I don't understand something, my friend can explain it to me. Also, we can practice together."
Medium
4. How do you remember new English words?
  • Write them down in a notebook.
  • Use flashcards.
  • Watch English cartoons.
  • Say them out loud 5 times.
"I write them in my notebook and read them."
"To remember new words, I make flashcards. I put the English word on one side and a drawing on the other. I practice them every day to improve my memory."
Medium
5. What do you do when you don't understand something at school?
  • Raise my hand and ask the teacher.
  • Ask the friend sitting next to me.
  • Search for it on the internet at home.
"I usually raise my hand and ask my teacher."
"When I don't understand, I try not to panic. First, I read the instructions again. If I am still confused, I ask my teacher to explain it in a different way."
Medium
6. Do games help you learn? How?
  • Games are not boring.
  • They make you think fast.
  • You want to win, so you try harder.
"Yes, because games are fun and not boring like reading."
"I believe games really help me learn. When I play a math game on my tablet, I don't feel like I am studying. I focus better because I want to win and get a high score."
Medium
7. Tell me about a time you made a mistake and learned from it.
  • Fell off a bike / skateboard.
  • Answered a question wrong in class.
  • Forgot homework at home.
"I forgot my homework once, so now I pack my bag at night."
"Once, I was riding my bike too fast on wet grass and I fell down. It hurt a little bit. But I learned my lesson. Now, I always ride slowly when it rains so I stay safe."
Opinion
8. In your opinion, is it easier to learn from a real teacher or a computer? Why?
  • Teacher: You can ask them questions, they smile.
  • Computer: It has cool videos, you can pause it.
"In my opinion, a real teacher is better because they are nice."
"In my opinion, it is easier to learn from a real teacher. A computer can show you videos, but it can't answer your specific questions. A teacher can see if you are confused and explain it better."
Opinion
9. Do you think learning outside (like in a park) is better than in a classroom?
  • Outside: Fresh air, lots of space, fun to look at trees.
  • Inside: Desks are comfortable, no bugs, a big whiteboard.
"I think outside is better because there is fresh air."
"I think learning outside is great for subjects like science because you can discover plants and bugs. But for math or writing, a classroom is better because you have a desk to focus."
Opinion
10. What is the most important thing to remember when learning something very hard?
  • Don't give up!
  • Practice makes perfect.
  • It's okay to ask for help.
"The most important thing is to never give up."
"When learning something hard, the most important thing is to remember that mistakes are okay. Everyone makes mistakes. You just have to be patient, keep practicing, and ask for help when you need it."

4. Role-Play Scenarios 🎭

Act it out! Read the situation and practice speaking.

Scenario 1: Asking for Help

Situation: You are in English class. The teacher gave you a worksheet, but you don't understand question number 3. Ask the teacher for help.

Roles: You (Student) & Teacher.

Student: "Excuse me, Mr./Mrs. [Name]?"

Teacher: "Yes? How can I help you?"

Student: "I don't understand question 3. Could you explain it to me, please?"

Teacher: "Of course! Let's look at it together..."

Scenario 2: Teaching a Game

Situation: You have a fun new video game. Your friend comes over and wants to play, but they don't know the rules. Teach them.

Roles: You (Expert) & Friend (Beginner).

Friend: "This game looks cool! How do you play?"

You: "It's easy! First, you press this button to jump."

Friend: "Oh, okay. What does the red button do?"

You: "The red button makes you run fast. Let's practice together!"

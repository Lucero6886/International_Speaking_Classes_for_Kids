🦁 Super Animals! 🐾

Let's talk about our favorite pets and wild animals!

👥 Kids (8-12) 📊 Mixed A1-A2 ⏱️ 60 Minutes 💻 Zoom Class

🎯 Today's Goals (By the end of this lesson, you can...):

  1. Name 10 common and wild animals clearly.
  2. Describe animals using cool action words and adjectives.
  3. Explain why you like an animal using "because".

⏱️ Zoom Teaching Flow:

  • 1. Warm-up (5 min)
  • 2. Vocabulary (8 min)
  • 3. Controlled Practice (8 min)
  • 4. Main Speaking (15 min)
  • 5. Breakout Role-play (12 min)
  • 6. Review Game (5 min)
  • 7. Reflection (5 min)
  • 8. Wrap-up (2 min)

🔥 1. Warm-up: Animal Chat!

ZOOM TIP Ask students to type simple answers in the chat, then select 2-3 students to unmute and speak.

1. Do you have a pet at home? 🐶🐱
A1 Core Yes, I have a dog. / No, I don't.
A2 Extended Yes, I have a cute cat. Her name is Bella.
2. What is your favorite animal in the world? 🐘🐅
A1 Core My favorite animal is a tiger.
A2 Extended I love dolphins because they are very smart.
3. Are you scared of any animals? 🕷️🐍
A1 Core Yes, I am scared of spiders.
A2 Extended Yes, I hate snakes because they are scary and dangerous.
4. Where can we go to see animals? 🏞️🦓
A1 Core We can go to the zoo or the park.
A2 Extended We can see animals in the zoo, the forest, or the pet shop.
5. If you could be an animal for one day, what would you be? 🦅🐒
A1 Core I want to be a bird.
A2 Extended I would be an eagle so I can fly high in the sky.

📚 2. Super Words!

ZOOM TIP Use "Repeat after me". Have students unmute their mics at the same time for a fun, noisy choral drill, then ask individuals.

Fluffy ☁️
(FLUF-fee)

Meaning: Covered with very soft hair or feathers.

A1
"My rabbit is very fluffy."
Wild 🌲
(WY-uld)

Meaning: Living in nature, not kept as a pet.

A1
"Lions are wild animals."
Climb 🧗
(KLIME)

Meaning: To use hands and feet to go up a tree or wall.

A1
"Monkeys can climb trees very fast."
Sharp Teeth 🦷
(SHARP TEETH)

Meaning: Teeth that easily cut meat and food.

A2
"Sharks have many sharp teeth."
Hunt 🐅
(HUNT)

Meaning: To chase and catch other animals for food.

A2
"Cheetahs hunt in the morning."
Colorful 🦜
(KUL-er-ful)

Meaning: Having many bright and beautiful colors.

A1
"Parrots are very colorful birds."
Dangerous ⚠️
(DAYN-jer-us)

Meaning: Not safe; able to hurt you or cause harm.

A2
"Don't touch the snake, it is dangerous."
Protect 🛡️
(pro-TEKT)

Meaning: To keep someone or something safe from danger.

A2
"Mother bears protect their babies."

🧩 3. Sentence Builders!

Teacher: Guide students to make sentences using these frames. Start easy, then add the challenge.

Level 1: I like... (A1)

"I like [animal] because it is [adjective]."

Example: "I like pandas because they are fluffy."

Your turn! Choose:
cats dogs tigers fast cute scary

Level 2: It can... (A2)

"My favorite animal is the [animal] because it can [action] and it has [body part]."

Example: "My favorite animal is the monkey because it can climb and it has a long tail."

Your turn! Choose:
run fast swim fly sharp teeth big wings

🎤 4. Let's Talk!

ZOOM TIP Assign students different numbers to prepare. Give them 1 minute of thinking time before calling on them.

1. Describe a dog. What does it look like? What can it do? 🐕
Easy fluffy run tail
A1: A dog has four legs and a tail. It can run.
A2: A dog is fluffy and friendly. It has a long tail and it can run very fast to catch a ball.
2. Which is better as a pet: a cat or a dog? Why? 🤔
Medium friendly quiet play with me
A1: I think a dog is better because it is fun.
A2: I think a cat is better because it is very quiet and fluffy. Dogs are too noisy.
3. Tell me about a wild animal. Where does it live? 🦁
Medium jungle hunt dangerous
A1: A lion lives in the wild. It is big and scary.
A2: A lion lives in the savanna. It is a dangerous wild animal and it hunts for meat.
4. What animal are you most like? Why? 🌟
Challenge lazy like a cat fast like a cheetah smart like a dolphin
A1: I am like a monkey because I like to play.
A2: I am like a cat because I like to sleep a lot on the weekend and I am very relaxed!

🎭 6. Breakout Room Role-Play

ZOOM TIP Put students in Breakout Rooms (Pairs) for 5-7 minutes. Assign Roles A and B.

🛒 Situation: At the Pet Shop

Goal: Student A wants to buy a pet. Student B helps them choose.

👦 Student A (Customer)

  • Say hello.
  • Say what kind of pet you want (fluffy, quiet, small?).
  • Ask: "What does it eat?"

👩‍🌾 Student B (Shop Worker)

  • Say hello. "Can I help you?"
  • Suggest an animal. "How about a [animal]?"
  • Explain why it's a good pet.

A: Hello! I want a new pet. I want a small and fluffy animal.

B: Hello! How about a rabbit? It is very fluffy and cute.

A: Oh, I like rabbits! What does it eat?

B: It eats carrots and vegetables!

🧑‍🏫 Teacher Support Box

Common Student Problems (Kids A1-A2):

  • Very short answers: Students might just say "Dog" instead of "I like dogs."
  • Forgot vocabulary: Kids easily forget verbs like "hunt" or "protect".
  • Pronunciation: Words like "dangerous" (3 syllables) can be tricky.

Gentle Correction Phrases:

  • "Good idea! Can you make a full sentence? 'My favorite is...'"
  • "That's right! Now, add a reason. Use 'because'."
  • "Great vocabulary! Let's say it together: dan-ger-ous."

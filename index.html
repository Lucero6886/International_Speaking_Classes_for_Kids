🍔 Yummy Food & Cooking Fun!

An A1-B1 Interactive English Speaking Lesson for Kids

🎯 Today's Goals:
  • Learn fun words to talk about tastes, cooking, and meals.
  • Describe your favorite foods and healthy eating habits.
  • Role-play ordering yummy food at a restaurant!

1. Let's Warm Up! 🍕

Are you hungry? Let's talk about food! Click to see an example.

🍟 1. What is your favorite food in the whole world?

"My absolute favorite food is pepperoni pizza with extra cheese!"

🥞 2. What did you eat for breakfast today?

"Today I ate sweet pancakes with honey and drank a glass of orange juice."

🌶️ 3. Do you like eating sweet food or spicy food?

"I love sweet food like chocolate, but I hate spicy food because it burns my mouth!"

🥦 4. What is a food that you really hate eating?

"I really hate eating broccoli and onions. They taste very yucky to me."

🍔 5. If you could eat burgers every day, would you?

"Yes, I would! But my mom says I need to eat vegetables to be strong and healthy."

2. Kitchen Words 🧑‍🍳

Let's learn new cooking words! Click "See Example" and the Speaker icon 🔊 to hear the word!

Delicious (adj.)
Very tasty and good to eat.
"Wow! This chocolate cake is absolutely delicious!"
Spicy (adj.)
Having a hot flavor that burns your mouth a little.
"I need some water! This chili pepper is too spicy!"
Chef (n.)
A person who cooks food as their job.
"When I grow up, I want to be a famous chef and open a restaurant."
Ingredients (n.)
The different foods you need to make a meal.
"We need eggs, flour, and sugar as ingredients for the cookies."
Recipe (n.)
Instructions telling you how to cook something.
"I am following my grandmother's secret recipe to make soup."
Order (v.)
To ask for food at a restaurant.
"Hello, I would like to order a large pizza and fries, please."
Healthy (adj.)
Good for your body and makes you strong.
"Eating fresh apples and green vegetables is very healthy."
Dessert (n.)
Sweet food eaten at the end of a meal.
"For dessert, we are having strawberry ice cream!"

3. Time to Speak! 🗣️

Let's answer these questions out loud. If you need help, open the Idea Bank!

1. What is your favorite snack to eat after school? Easy
💡 Idea Bank:
  • Sweet snacks: Cookies, chocolate, a piece of fruit, ice cream.
  • Salty snacks: Potato chips, cheese, sandwiches, crackers.
"My favorite snack after school is a chocolate chip cookie and milk."
"When I come home from school, I am usually very hungry. My absolute favorite snack is a cold glass of milk and some warm chocolate chip cookies. Sometimes I also eat an apple if I want something healthy."
2. Can you cook anything? What can you make? Easy
💡 Idea Bank:
  • Simple foods: Boiled eggs, instant noodles, toast with jam.
  • Helping mom/dad: Washing vegetables, mixing the cake batter, setting the table.
"Yes, I can make a sandwich and boil eggs all by myself."
"I am not a master chef yet, but I can make a few simple things! I know how to make toast with strawberry jam, and I can cook instant noodles. On the weekends, I help my mom mix the ingredients for pancakes."
3. What do you usually have for dinner with your family? Easy
💡 Idea Bank:
  • Vietnamese food: Rice, hot soup, fried fish, boiled vegetables.
  • Western food: Spaghetti, roasted chicken, pizza.
"We usually have rice, chicken, and a big bowl of vegetable soup."
"For dinner, my family usually eats traditional Vietnamese food. We always have a big bowl of white rice, some fried pork or fish, and hot vegetable soup. My mom is a great cook, so dinner is always delicious!"
4. Tell me about a time you went to a restaurant. What did you eat? Medium
💡 Idea Bank:
  • Where: A pizza place, a sushi restaurant, a fast-food shop.
  • What you ate: A huge slice of pizza, crispy fried chicken, yummy chocolate dessert.
"Last week we went to KFC and I ate fried chicken and french fries."
"For my birthday last month, my parents took me to a fantastic Italian restaurant. I ordered a huge spaghetti with meatballs, and it tasted amazing. After we finished our meal, the waiter brought me a slice of chocolate cake for dessert."
5. Why is it important to eat fruits and vegetables? Medium
💡 Idea Bank:
  • Body: Makes you grow tall, makes your muscles strong, gives you energy.
  • Health: Vitamins, stops you from getting sick, good for your brain.
"It is important because they have vitamins that make us strong and healthy."
"I know that fruits and vegetables are very important for our bodies. They are full of healthy vitamins that stop us from getting sick. If we eat a lot of carrots and apples, we will have lots of energy to play sports and grow very tall."
6. If you opened a restaurant, what kind of food would you sell? Medium
💡 Idea Bank:
  • Type of place: A sweet bakery, a spicy Mexican shop, a magical burger place.
  • Special menu: Rainbow ice cream, burgers as big as your head, flying pancakes.
"I would open a dessert shop and sell giant ice cream cones and cupcakes."
"That sounds so fun! If I opened a restaurant, it would be a magical dessert cafe. The menu would have rainbow cupcakes, chocolate fountains, and milkshakes that glow in the dark! I would be the head chef and invent new sweets every day."
7. What is the strangest food you have ever tasted? Medium
💡 Idea Bank:
  • Weird foods: Snails, frog meat, a strange fruit (like Durian), bugs.
  • Feeling: Scared to try it, smelled funny, surprisingly tasty, very gross.
"The strangest food I ever ate was Durian fruit. It smelled terrible but tasted sweet!"
"Last year, my uncle gave me something very strange to eat. It was a cooked snail! I was very scared to try it because it looked slippery and weird. When I finally took a bite, it was chewy and tasted a bit like chicken. It wasn't too bad!"
8. Do you think kids should be allowed to eat candy every day? Opinion
💡 Idea Bank:
  • No: Bad for your teeth (cavities), too much sugar makes you crazy, not healthy.
  • Yes, but: Only one small piece after dinner, only if you brush your teeth well.
"No, because eating candy every day will give you bad teeth and a stomach ache."
"Even though I love sweets, I don't think kids should eat candy every single day. Sugar is very bad for our teeth and can give us painful cavities. Also, if we eat too much sugar, we might get a stomach ache. Candy should be a special treat for the weekend."
9. Is it better to eat food cooked at home or eat at a restaurant? Opinion
💡 Idea Bank:
  • Home: Healthier, cheaper, family time, you know the ingredients.
  • Restaurant: Fun experience, you don't have to wash the dishes, fancy food.
"I think eating at home is better because my mom's cooking is very healthy."
"In my opinion, eating at home is much better. When we cook at home, we know exactly what ingredients we are using, so it is much healthier and cleaner. Also, cooking together is a fun way to spend time with my family. Restaurants are fun, but they are expensive."
10. Should parents force their children to eat vegetables if they hate them? Opinion
💡 Idea Bank:
  • No forcing: It makes kids cry, they will hate vegetables forever.
  • Creative ideas: Hide veggies in a soup, put cheese on top, make them look like funny faces.
"No, they shouldn't force them. Instead, parents should cook the vegetables in a yummy way."
"I don't think parents should yell or force kids to eat vegetables because it makes kids feel sad. Instead, parents should try to be creative! They can hide carrots inside a tasty soup, or put melted cheese on top of broccoli to make it delicious. Cooking together also helps kids want to eat it."

4. Let's Act! 🎭

Grab a partner and practice these fun restaurant situations.

🍔 Scenario A: The Hungry Customer

Situation: You are very hungry and go to a fast-food restaurant. You want to order a big meal. Talk to the waiter.

Student A: Hungry Customer Student B: Happy Waiter
Waiter: Welcome to Happy Burger! What would you like to order today?
Customer: Hello! I am very hungry. I would like a giant cheeseburger, please.
Waiter: Would you like some fries and a drink with that?
Customer: Yes, large fries and a cold cola, please.
Waiter: Okay! That will be 10 dollars, please.
Customer: Here is the money. Thank you!

🦕 Scenario B: The Toy in the Soup!

Situation: You are eating at a fancy restaurant, but you find a plastic toy dinosaur inside your hot soup! Call the Chef.

Student A: Shocked Customer Student B: Confused Chef
Customer: Excuse me, Mr. Chef! Come here, please!
Chef: Hello! Is there a problem with your food?
Customer: Yes! Look! There is a plastic dinosaur swimming in my soup!
Chef: Oh my goodness! I am so sorry! How did a dinosaur get in there?
Customer: I don't know, but I cannot eat this!
Chef: My little boy was playing in the kitchen today. I will make you a new, delicious soup for free!
